Role: Lead Designer/Programmer/Writer

Developed In: Javascript

Jax Tallon, the Hoot Corps' first IncognitOwl, embarks on a mission to use reconnaissance rather than violence to solve the dispute between Owls and Bats. Set up traps and plan missions by day, then execute infiltrations by night. Learn to utilize both the day's point and click tower-defense style mechanics and the night's arrow-key sneaking controls to become the world's first IncognitOwl.

IncognitOwl was made with a team of 4 during my Sophomore Year at UCSC, for David Wessman's Game Design Experience class. From a concept that originates back to a joke I made outside a grocery store, IncognitOwl quickly grew into so much more as a result of several late nights. Our passion and drive for making this game made working in a bigger team for the first time a breeze. To learn more about our process check out the video to the left, or try the game for your self below!

Role: Level Designer/Scrum Master

Tools:

Klash with your friends in this 3D combat arena and compete for the title of "Top Krab"!

Krab Klashers was a game developed in a software engineering class at UCSC, utilizing a Scrum development methodolgy. Our goal for the project was to familiarize ourselves with 3D and multiplayer game production, as it is a foreign field for all of us. We chose to keep the game's mechanics relatively simple, yet entertaining. By keeping the scope under control, the 6 of us were able to gain a ton of experience with several areas of game design we had yet to explore.

Role: Lead Designer/Writer

Developed In: Unity2D (C#)

Two lovestruck cats are about to enter a long-distance relationship, and the pesky phone bills aren't cheap. Earn money through a series of odd jobs to try and make things work in this brief, yet heart-wrenching story-game.

What Do We Do Meow was developed at the 2015 Global Game Jam. It was the first jam for all 5 of us, but I'm pretty proud of what we came up with in a meager 48 hours. I learned a lot about the importance of planning/storyboarding, as well as how to effectively create under pressue. It was an unforgettable experience, and I'll definitely be attending many more jams in the future!

Role: Designer/Programmer

Developed In: GameMaker

After falling into a deep sleep, a small dinosaur finds himself stuck at the bottom of a multi-layered dream. And what better way to wake up from a dream than by activating a robot elephant or being mauled by a tiger? The dino's subconscious mind is full of bizarre and puzzling scenarios in which he must find a way to shock himself awake. That is, awake into the next layer. Will he ever escape this Dreamlock?

This game was a game project I took on Freshman Year for Brenda Romero's Foundations of Interactive Game Design class at UCSC. My partner and I used GameMaker to create the game in a little over 8 weeks. We went on to receive an Honorable Mention for the game's charming aesthetic and dreamlike art-style. If interested in learning more about Dreamlock, feel free to check out my commentary video on the right, or download the game below!
